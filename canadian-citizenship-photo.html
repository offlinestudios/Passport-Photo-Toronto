Canadian Citizenship Photo Requirements

IRCC has specific requirements for citizenship photos. Our service ensures your photos will comply with all these requirements:

  • Size: 50mm x 70mm (standard Canadian citizenship photo size)
  • Recent photograph taken within the last 6 months
  • Color photo with a white or light-colored background
  • Full face view directly facing the camera
  • Neutral facial expression with mouth closed
  • Eyes open and clearly visible
  • No shadows on the face or background
  • No glasses (as of 2021 IRCC regulations)
  • No head coverings (except for religious reasons)
  • Proper lighting with no red-eye effect

Canadian Citizenship Application Information

While we specialize in citizenship photos, here's some general information about Canadian citizenship applications:

  • You must be a permanent resident and have lived in Canada for at least 3 out of the last 5 years (1,095 days)
  • You must have filed your taxes for at least 3 years during the 5-year period
  • You must pass a citizenship test if you're between 18 and 54 years old
  • You must prove your language skills in English or French
  • Processing times vary, but typically take 12-18 months for standard applications
  • Two identical photos are required for the application
  • The photographer must sign, date, and print their name on the back of one photo

Common Citizenship Photo Mistakes to Avoid

Many citizenship applications are delayed or rejected due to photo issues. Common mistakes include:

  • Incorrect photo size
  • Shadows on face or background
  • Wearing glasses (no longer permitted in citizenship photos)
  • Improper facial expression (smiling, frowning, etc.)
  • Poor lighting or contrast
  • Photos taken too long ago (must be within 6 months)
  • Missing photographer information on the back of the photo

Our professional service helps you avoid these common pitfalls, ensuring your application proceeds smoothly.
