Immigration Medical Photo Requirements

IRCC has specific requirements for photos used in immigration medical examinations. Our service ensures your photos will comply with all these requirements:

  • Size: 50mm x 70mm (standard immigration medical photo size)
  • Recent photograph taken within the last 6 months
  • Color photo with a white or light-colored background
  • Full face view directly facing the camera
  • Neutral facial expression
  • Eyes open and clearly visible
  • No shadows on the face or background
  • No glasses (unless medically required)
  • No head coverings (except for religious reasons)
  • Proper lighting with no red-eye effect

About Immigration Medical Examinations

An Immigration Medical Examination (IME) is a mandatory health screening for many Canadian immigration applicants. Here's some general information about immigration medicals:

  • Required for most permanent residence applications
  • Required for some temporary residence applications (work permits, study permits)
  • Must be conducted by an IRCC-designated panel physician
  • Typically includes a physical examination, blood tests, urinalysis, and chest X-ray
  • Results are valid for 12 months
  • Photos are required for identification purposes during the examination

Immigration Programs Requiring Medical Examinations

Our immigration medical photos are suitable for various Canadian immigration programs, including:

  • Express Entry (Federal Skilled Worker, Federal Skilled Trades, Canadian Experience Class)
  • Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)
  • Family Sponsorship
  • Refugee and Humanitarian Programs
  • Temporary Foreign Worker Program
  • International Mobility Program
  • Study Permits (in some cases)
  • Super Visa applications

Immigration Medical Information

While we specialize in immigration medical photos, here's some general advice for your medical examination:

  • Book your medical examination well in advance of your application submission
  • Bring your passport or other government-issued photo ID
  • Bring your medical photo
  • Bring any prescription medications you're currently taking
  • Bring your glasses or contact lenses if you wear them
  • Bring your medical history records if available
  • Be prepared to provide urine and blood samples

For the most current information about Canadian immigration medical examinations, please visit the official IRCC website.
