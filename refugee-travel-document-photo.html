Refugee Travel Document Photo Requirements

IRCC has specific requirements for refugee travel document photos. Our service ensures your photos will comply with all these requirements:

  • Size: 50mm x 70mm (standard Canadian travel document photo size)
  • Recent photograph taken within the last 6 months
  • Color photo with a white or light-colored background
  • Full face view directly facing the camera
  • Neutral facial expression
  • Eyes open and clearly visible
  • No shadows on the face or background
  • No glasses (unless medically required)
  • No head coverings (except for religious reasons)
  • Proper lighting with no red-eye effect

About Refugee Travel Documents

A Refugee Travel Document is an identity document issued to residents of Canada who have protected person status, including convention refugees and persons in need of protection. Here's some general information about refugee travel documents:

  • Allows refugees and protected persons to travel outside Canada
  • Serves as a passport substitute for those who cannot obtain a passport from their country of nationality
  • Valid for up to 5 years (cannot be extended)
  • Not valid for travel to the country of claimed persecution
  • Must be applied for before leaving Canada
  • Two identical photos are required for the application

Eligibility for Refugee Travel Documents

You may be eligible for a Canadian Refugee Travel Document if you are:

  • A protected person (including convention refugees) in Canada
  • A permanent resident who cannot get a passport from your country of nationality because you are a stateless person
  • A permanent resident who cannot get a passport from your country of nationality for a valid reason

Refugee Travel Document Application Information

While we specialize in refugee travel document photos, here's some general information about the application process:

  • Applications can be submitted online or by mail
  • Processing times vary, but typically take 4-8 weeks
  • The application fee is approximately $120-160 for adults
  • You must submit two identical photos with your application
  • One photo must be signed by the guarantor on the back
  • You should apply at least two months before your planned travel date

For the most current information about Canadian Refugee Travel Document applications, please visit the official IRCC website.
